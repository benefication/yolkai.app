Introduction to Video Generation Prompts

Creating effective video generation prompts is an art that combines creativity with technical precision. This guide will help you craft prompts that produce high-quality, engaging videos using AI video generation tools.

Basic Prompt Structure

A well-structured video prompt should include:

  • Subject: What or who is the main focus of the video
  • Action: What is happening in the scene
  • Setting: Where the action takes place
  • Style: Visual style and mood
  • Camera work: Shot type and movement

Example Basic Prompt:

"A golden retriever running through a sunlit meadow, with wildflowers swaying in the breeze, shot with a smooth tracking camera movement, warm and cheerful atmosphere"

Advanced Prompting Techniques

1. Detailed Scene Description

Include specific details about lighting, weather, time of day, and environmental elements to create more realistic and engaging videos.

Detailed Scene Example:

"Create a cinematic video of a vintage red convertible driving down a winding mountain road at sunset. The car should be moving smoothly with the golden hour light casting long shadows. Include details like dust particles in the air, the sound of the engine, and panoramic mountain views in the background. Use a combination of tracking shots and aerial drone footage."

2. Character and Object Specifications

When including people or objects, be specific about their appearance, clothing, and behavior.

Character Description Example:

"A young woman in a flowing white dress walking barefoot on a beach at sunrise. She has long brown hair blowing in the wind, and she's looking out at the ocean with a peaceful expression. The waves are gentle, and seagulls are flying overhead."

3. Camera Movement and Cinematography

Specify camera movements to create dynamic and professional-looking videos.

Camera Movement Description Use Case
Tracking shot Camera follows the subject Action sequences, following characters
Pan Horizontal camera movement Landscape reveals, establishing shots
Tilt Vertical camera movement Revealing tall objects, dramatic reveals
Zoom Camera lens adjustment Focus on details, emotional moments

Negative Prompts

Negative prompts help specify what elements to avoid in your video generation. Instead of using instructive language, describe what you don't want to see.

Negative Prompt Example:

Good: "urban background, man-made structures, dark atmosphere"
Avoid: "no walls" or "don't show buildings"

Aspect Ratios

Choose the appropriate aspect ratio for your video based on its intended use:

Aspect Ratio Description Best For
16:9 (Widescreen) Standard landscape format YouTube, TV, desktop viewing
9:16 (Portrait) Vertical format TikTok, Instagram Stories, mobile

Image-to-Video Generation

You can bring static images to life by using image-to-video capabilities. When using reference images:

  • Ensure actions align with subjects in the image
  • Specify which character performs actions when multiple subjects are present
  • Use distinguishing details to differentiate between characters

Image-to-Video Example:

"The man in the red hat says, 'Where is the rabbit?' Then the woman in the green dress next to him replies, 'There, in the woods.'"

Best Practices

  1. Be Specific: Include detailed descriptions of visual elements
  2. Use Action Words: Describe movement and behavior clearly
  3. Set the Mood: Specify lighting, atmosphere, and emotional tone
  4. Consider Duration: Think about the pacing and length of your video
  5. Test and Iterate: Refine your prompts based on results

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Being too vague or generic in descriptions
  • Including conflicting instructions
  • Overcomplicating the prompt with too many elements
  • Not specifying camera work or movement
  • Ignoring lighting and atmospheric details

More tips for writing prompts

The following tips help you write prompts that generate your videos:

  • Use descriptive language: Use adjectives and adverbs to paint a clear picture for Veo.
  • Provide context: If necessary, include background information to help your model understand what you want.
  • Reference specific artistic styles: If you have a particular aesthetic in mind, reference specific artistic styles or art movements.
  • Utilize prompt engineering tools: Consider exploring prompt engineering tools or resources to help you refine your prompts and achieve optimal results.
  • Enhance the facial details in your personal and group images: Specify facial details as a focus of the photo like using the word portrait in the prompt.
